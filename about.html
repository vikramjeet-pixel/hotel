Our Heritage

A timeless story of Tudor craftsmanship, family legacy, and the art of hospitality.

The Tale

A Tudor Legacy

Nestled in the heart of Shakespeare’s Warwickshire, Kings Court Hotel began its life in 1580 as a humble Tudor farmhouse. Built from local stone and oak beams that still stand strong today, the original structure was a testament to the enduring craftsmanship of the Elizabethan era.

Through centuries of change, from a hardworking farmstead to a private manor retreat, the estate has always been a place of gathering. The ancient oak tree in our courtyard, rumored to be older than the house itself, has witnessed generations of travelers finding rest within these walls.

Preserving History
Through the Ages

Our Journey

1580

The Foundation

The original farmhouse is constructed. Tudor beams and stone fireplaces from this era remain key features of our Courtyard Rooms.

1850

The Expansion

The estate expands into a private manor house. The gardens are landscaped, adding the rose pathways guests enjoy today.

1985

Becoming a Hotel

Kings Court opens its doors to the public, transforming into a boutique hotel while carefully preserving its historic character.

Present

Modern Luxury

With award-winning dining and redesigned suites, we continue to honor our past while offering world-class comfort to modern travelers.
