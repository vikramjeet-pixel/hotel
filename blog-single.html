A Perfect Day in Stratford-upon-Avon

December 2024 8 min read 3 miles from hotel

Shakespeare's birthplace is one of England's most beloved destinations, and staying at Kings Court Hotel places you just a short drive â€” or a pleasant 45-minute stroll along the river â€” from the heart of this historic market town. Here's our insider guide to making the most of your visit.

Morning: History & Heritage

Start your day at Shakespeare's Birthplace on Henley Street, the charming half-timbered house where the world's greatest playwright was born in 1564. The museum brings Tudor life vividly to life with interactive exhibits and costumed guides.

From there, it's a gentle walk through the town centre to Hall's Croft, the elegant Jacobean home of Shakespeare's daughter Susanna. The tranquil gardens here are perfect for a quiet morning moment.

Don't Miss: The Guild Chapel

Often overlooked by visitors, the Guild Chapel on Chapel Lane contains recently restored medieval wall paintings that are truly extraordinary â€” and admission is free.

Afternoon: Riverside & Theatre

After lunch, take a leisurely walk along the banks of the River Avon. The Bancroft Gardens offer beautiful riverside views, and you can hire a rowing boat or watch the narrow boats glide by.

"There is something magical about standing on the banks of the Avon at twilight, the Royal Shakespeare Theatre glowing against the darkening sky. It reminds you why this place has inspired artists for centuries."

If you're visiting between March and January, don't miss a performance at the Royal Shakespeare Company. The RSC stages world-class productions of Shakespeare and contemporary works. We recommend booking well in advance for popular shows.

Our Top Tip

The RSC offers backstage tours that let you explore the working theatre, including the costume department and rehearsal spaces. Book the 10:15 AM tour to combine it with your morning explorations.

Evening: Dining & Strolling

As the sun sets, Stratford transforms into a magical scene. The historic streets glow under gentle lamplight, and the restaurants come alive with the buzz of conversation.

Of course, the finest dining in the area awaits you back at Kings Court. The Garden Restaurant offers a seasonal tasting menu that showcases the best of Warwickshire's producers, paired with wines from our carefully curated cellar.
